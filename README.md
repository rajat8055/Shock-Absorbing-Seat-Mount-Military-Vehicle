# Design and Structural Analysis of Shock-Absorbing Seat Mount for Military Vehicles

## Project Overview
This project presents the complete design and finite element structural analysis of a shock-absorbing seat mounting assembly intended for military vehicles operating on rugged terrain. The objective is to reduce direct transmission of impact shocks, vibration loads, and sudden jerks from the vehicle chassis to the occupant seating system.

The developed model was engineered using CAD assembly techniques and validated through ANSYS Workbench structural simulations to ensure durability, deformation resistance, and occupant safety.


## Objective
- Minimize impact force transfer to vehicle occupant.
- Improve vibration damping through mechanical seat support arrangement.
- Evaluate structural strength under high loading conditions.
- Validate fatigue life and stress sustainability of mounting members.


## Software and Tools Used
- SolidWorks (3D CAD Assembly Design)
- ANSYS Workbench (Static Structural Analysis)
- Finite Element Meshing
- Engineering Data Material Assignment
- STEP Geometry Integration


## Engineering Analysis Performed
- Equivalent Von-Mises Stress Analysis
- Total Deformation Observation
- Directional Deformation Analysis
- Mesh Validation
- Contact Region Study
- Fatigue Tool Assessment


## Files Included
- Mechanical Structural Report PDF
- CAD STEP Assembly Model
- ANSYS Workbench Project File
- Meshed STL Structural Geometry


## Industrial Applications
- Military Vehicle Occupant Safety
- Defense Seating Mechanism Design
- Shock Isolation Systems
- Structural Product Development
- CAD/CAE Mechanical Validation


## Project Outcome
The simulated seat mount assembly demonstrated controlled deformation, acceptable equivalent stress distribution, and enhanced impact energy dissipation capability under applied loading conditions. The design provides improved rider protection and mechanical reliability for severe military operating environments.
